<p>When the heat hits, getting dressed can feel like a trade-off between looking good and staying comfortable. But you really can have both. The secret to staying stylish in the heat isn't more clothing — it's smarter clothing: the right fabrics, relaxed shapes, and a cooling color palette. Here's how to look put-together even when the temperature climbs.</p>

<h2>Start with breathable fabrics</h2>
<p>Fabric is the foundation of hot-weather style. Natural, breathable materials let air move and wick away moisture, so you feel cooler and look fresher. Reach for:</p>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Linen</strong> — the ultimate summer fabric; airy, relaxed, and effortlessly chic.</li>
  <li><strong>Cotton</strong> — soft, breathable, and endlessly versatile.</li>
  <li><strong>Chambray and lightweight denim</strong> — for a little structure without the heat.</li>
  <li><strong>Flowing viscose or rayon</strong> — light and drapey for dresses and skirts.</li>
</ul>
<p>Avoid heavy synthetics that trap heat and moisture — they're the fastest way to feel sticky.</p>

<h2>Embrace loose, relaxed silhouettes</h2>
<p>Tight clothing clings and traps heat, so let your outfits breathe. Loose, flowing shapes keep air circulating against your skin and read as intentional and elegant. Think relaxed midi dresses, wide-leg trousers, floaty skirts, and oversized shirts. If you love a fitted look, balance it out — pair a slim tank with billowy trousers, or a fitted skirt with an airy blouse.</p>

<h2>Choose a cooling color palette</h2>
<p>Light colors reflect sunlight while dark colors absorb it, so your palette genuinely affects how warm you feel. Whites, creams, pastels, and soft neutrals feel fresh and stay cooler in direct sun. They also photograph beautifully in bright summer light. Save your deepest, darkest shades for the evening or cooler days.</p>

<h2>Easy hot-weather outfit formulas</h2>
<p>When it's too hot to think, lean on these no-fail combinations:</p>
<ul>
  <li><strong>The breezy dress.</strong> One flowing midi or sundress and flat sandals — done.</li>
  <li><strong>The linen set.</strong> Matching shorts or trousers with a coordinating top looks pulled-together with zero effort.</li>
  <li><strong>Tank + wide-leg trousers.</strong> A fitted tank tucked into airy trousers is elevated and cool.</li>
  <li><strong>Shorts + oversized shirt.</strong> Tailored shorts with a loose button-down, sleeves rolled, is easy summer polish.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Smart styling details</h2>
<p>Small choices make hot-weather outfits work harder. Open-toe sandals and slides keep your feet cool. A wide-brim hat and sunglasses add instant polish while protecting you from the sun. A straw bag brings a summer feel to anything. And if you're heading somewhere with air conditioning, tuck a light layer — a linen shirt or a fine-knit cardigan — into your bag for the temperature swing.</p>

<h2>Keep accessories light</h2>
<p>In the heat, less is more. Skip heavy layers of jewelry that stick to warm skin, and choose a few delicate pieces instead — thin gold chains, small hoops, a simple bracelet. Natural materials like straw, raffia, and canvas feel seasonally right and keep the whole look breezy.</p>

<h2>The takeaway</h2>
<p>Staying stylish in the heat comes down to a simple strategy: breathable fabrics like linen and cotton, loose and flowing silhouettes, a light cooling color palette, and easy outfit formulas you can throw on without thinking. Add a hat, sandals, and a few delicate accessories, and you'll look effortlessly chic — no matter how high the temperature climbs.</p>
