<p>The secret to looking great in your clothes isn't a trend or a price tag — it's <strong>fit</strong>. Dressing for your body shape simply means choosing cuts that balance your proportions and highlight what you love. These are guidelines, not rules: wear what makes you feel confident. Here's a simple starting point.</p>

<h2>First, find your general shape</h2>
<p>Most people loosely fit one of these — and many are a mix. Don't overthink it; it's just a starting point.</p>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Hourglass</strong> — shoulders and hips balanced, defined waist.</li>
  <li><strong>Pear (triangle)</strong> — hips wider than shoulders.</li>
  <li><strong>Apple (round)</strong> — weight carried around the middle, great legs.</li>
  <li><strong>Rectangle</strong> — shoulders, waist, and hips fairly even.</li>
  <li><strong>Inverted triangle</strong> — shoulders wider than hips.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Simple styling ideas by shape</h2>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Hourglass:</strong> Play up your waist. Wrap dresses, high-waisted bottoms, and fitted (not baggy) pieces show off your natural balance.</li>
  <li><strong>Pear:</strong> Draw the eye up. Detailed or brighter tops, off-shoulder necklines, and darker, straight or bootcut bottoms balance the hips.</li>
  <li><strong>Apple:</strong> Elongate and define. V-necks, empire waists, flowy tops over fitted bottoms, and showing off your legs work beautifully.</li>
  <li><strong>Rectangle:</strong> Create curves. Peplum tops, belted waists, ruffles, and layering add shape and definition.</li>
  <li><strong>Inverted triangle:</strong> Add volume below. A-line skirts, wide-leg trousers, and simpler necklines balance broader shoulders.</li>
</ul>

<h2>The rules that matter more than shape</h2>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Fit first.</strong> The right size that skims your body always looks better than "on-trend" that pulls or bags.</li>
  <li><strong>Tailoring is magic.</strong> A small nip at the waist or hem can transform an inexpensive piece.</li>
  <li><strong>Define a waist</strong> — a belt or a tucked-in top instantly makes most outfits look intentional.</li>
  <li><strong>Wear what you love.</strong> Confidence flatters every shape more than any styling tip.</li>
</ul>

<h2>The takeaway</h2>
<p>Dressing for your shape is really about understanding <em>your</em> proportions and choosing pieces that make you feel great. Use these as a loose guide, then break every rule the moment something makes you feel amazing.</p>
